Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

if it's more than five years old, shows visible signs of wear and tear like cracks, scratches, or damaged padding, has been involved in a crash, or if the interior is significantly worn or coming apart, regardless of its appearance

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

it's not recommended to ride it immediately without first performing thorough checks

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

When riding your bike, always wear a helmet, ride with traffic flow, use hand signals when turning or stopping, stay aware of your surroundings, yield to pedestrians, ride in a predictable manner, check your blind spots before changing lanes, and follow all local traffic laws applicable to cyclists; this includes using designated bike lanes when available and riding in the center of the lane when necessary to be visible to other road users
